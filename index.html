Introduction

In a microservice architecture, a microservice needs a way to locate other microservices. Once located, a microservice needs a way to send data between the microservices.

Baker Street provides an integrated solution for:

  • Service discovery: locating an available instance of a microservice
  • Load balancing: automatically distributing requests to a particular type of microservice against all available instances of that microservice
  • Upgrades and testing: automatically add/remove new versions of a microservice to the pool, which lets you transparently test and upgrade a version without rolling it out all at once.

What else you could use?

There are a number of different service discovery frameworks available. Why Baker Street?

  • Baker Street installs in just a few minutes. Try it!
  • None of these frameworks, with the exception of SmartStack provide integrated routing. Yes, you could write your own. But why bother?
  • Most of the frameworks focus on consistency, not availability. But what you really care about is availability. When a microservice dies, you don't need or want your service discovery to come to consensus about whether or not it's dead -- you want to just propagate this information ASAP.
